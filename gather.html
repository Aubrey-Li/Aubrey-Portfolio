Startup of our choice--Gather--helps people and team coordinate with smart and automated communication by deep integration with Slack, so employees could connect with each other more easily with more streamlined message sending based on special events such as onboarding/offboarding, work anniversary, birthdays, etc.

We believe that those who work in large company and have complicated projects that need collaboration between departments would benefit a lot from this app.

üé® Design Principles & Inspirations:

In our design, we wish that the main functionalities be centered around two modules--workspace & personal dashboard.

Workspace: forming connections with company employees, automated slack message management system, team collaboration on projects.

Personal Dashboard: focuses on sorting out personal tasks and duties from workspace.

To release stress in the workplace, we want to use color palettes with low saturation colors, but have primary colors to highlight important messages. Increase organization and tidiness of layout, use hierarchical navbar. Our inspiration of design (when brainstorming) comes from Slack, Notion, Trello, Cloze, and Facebook Workplace.




User Testing

üìå Testing and Analysis

Homepage overview task

Task Description:

Explore the homepage and describe your thoughts.

Users‚Äô reactions:

Some users could identify the prototype as a platform that is designed for a company-wide dashboard, which is consistent with our design idea. However, since not everyone of them were familiar with Slack, it is hard for them to identify the main purpose of the slack dashboard.

Connection task

Task Description:

Main focus: Build new connections within the company, and at the same time keep track of existing connections.

Subtask 1: Build new connections through the searching functionality, find the software engineer L.R., and add her to existing connections.

Subtask 2: Find out more information about one of your existing connections through exploring your connection tree.

Users‚Äô reactions:

User testing: Among the three users we tested, most of the users were able to find L.R. and add her to existing connections, and also find Jose.G to check out his email. This result validates our intention of building the page in two parts so users can both find new connections and also manage existing connections. One user also said she liked the layout of the bubble personal connections.

Errors: Some users were having some trouble finding the specific persons in the task. This error might be a result of not knowing which page is the person‚Äôs existing connections and where to find new connections. To improve upon this user error, we might consider adding text instructions within the search bar indicating that the search bar allows user to search for both new and existing connections.

Calendar task

Task Description:

Check out events happening on a given day in your personal dashboard, as well as its participants on the calendar.

Users‚Äô reactions:

Most users could quickly found the events happening on a given day which was highlighted and clicked on the drop down menu to check the participants. All of the users completed this task, although one of them took some time finding the calendar.

Project task

Task Description:

This task aims to allow audiences to check the progress of different projects.

Users‚Äô reactions:

All of the three users successfully navigated to the main project page through the menu bar on the left. The whole process moved smoothly and no one made any mistakes.
